GREEN POWER

Energy

Energy is the ability to perform work, which is the foundation of modern life. Energy is available in various forms such as electricity, heat, movement, and radiation. Energy can be converted from one form to another using certain tools and techniques.

Energy is the ability to do work

According to the laws of conservation of energy, energy can neither be created nor destroyed but can only be converted from one form to another. The SI unit of energy is Joule.

Energy Start

Humans have used energy since ancient times. The first human was dependent on the muscular energy of hunting prey and agricultural activities. As civilizations evolved, man began to discover other sources of energy:

In the 19th century, the use of fossil fuels (coal, oil, natural gas) began widely, especially after the invention of steam machinery.

Energy Impactergy

Energy has great effects on everyday life and economies:

1. Positive

  • Improve the standard of living and provide basic services such as lighting, heating, and mobility.
  • Contribute to the development of industries and enhance productivity.
  • Renewable energy contributes to environmental conservation.

2. Negative

  • Conventional energy such as fossil fuels contributes to air pollution and climate change.
  • Unsustainable energy consumption can lead to resource scarcity and high production costs.

Necessity to learn the Basics in Energy

1. Increasing environmental awareness: Understanding how energy choices affect the environment can help individuals and communities make more sustainable decisions.

2. Keeping up with technological developments: Energy is an essential part of many modern te.chnological innovations. Learning the basics contributes to understanding and developing these technologies

3. Sustainability: Learning how to use energy sources effectively contributes to resource conservation for future generations.

4. Improving efficiency: Learning how to save energy in our daily lives helps reduce financial costs
